# EduVoyage Landing Page PRD (Design-First, No Code)

## 1) Product Goal and Experience Intent

EduVoyage landing page should feel like a guided learning journey, not a generic SaaS homepage.

Primary goal:
- Convert first-time visitors into signed-in learners who start a course.

Secondary goals:
- Build trust in AI quality and learning outcomes.
- Show clear end-to-end workflow from goal to certificate.
- Highlight career impact, not only content generation.

Core CTA:
- Primary: Start Learning Free
- Secondary: Watch Demo
- Tertiary: Explore Features

Tone:
- Confident, modern, mentor-like.
- Strong clarity, no hype fluff.

---

## 2) Information Architecture and Page Flow

Single-page flow with sticky navigation and section-based scroll storytelling.

Section order:
1. Header / Navigation
2. Hero (Mission + Core CTA + Demo trigger)
3. Trust Strip (stats, logos, social proof)
4. Problem to Solution Narrative
5. End-to-End Workflow Timeline
6. Feature Deep Dive Grid
7. Career and Skill Layer Spotlight
8. Quiz and Certificate Outcomes
9. Product Preview / Interactive Workspace Showcase
10. Testimonials / Learner Outcomes
11. Pricing and Free vs Premium Value
12. Final CTA Band
13. Footer

Why this order:
- Visitor first understands why this product exists.
- Then sees how it works in sequence.
- Then sees proof and outcomes.
- Then makes a low-friction conversion decision.

## 3) Detailed Section Blueprint (Text-First Layout)

Content:

- Nav items: Home, How It Works, Features, Outcomes, Pricing, Contact

## 3.4 Problem to Solution Narrative

Purpose:
- Make visitor feel understood, then present EduVoyage as solution.

Layout:
- Two-column story block.
- Left: Problem cards (content overload, no structure, no measurable outcomes).
- Right: Matching solution cards (AI course builder, guided chapters, quizzes + certificates).

Copy blocks:
- Problem title: Too Much Content, Too Little Direction
- Solution title: One System From Intention to Completion

UX scroll reaction:
- As user scrolls, each problem card crossfades into solution card.
- Thin progress rail fills downward, signaling transformation.

---

1. Define your learning goal
2. Generate AI course layout
3. Learn chapter-by-chapter
4. Watch curated videos
5. Complete smart quizzes
6. Unlock certificate

- Progress line animates from 0% to 100% as user scrolls through section.

## 4) Scroll Storyboard (How UX Reacts While User Scrolls)

Scroll phase A (0% to 15%):
- Hero text reveal, dashboard parallax, CTA entrance.

Scroll phase B (15% to 35%):
- Trust strip enters, then problem-to-solution morph.

Scroll phase C (35% to 60%):
- Pinned workflow timeline with active step progression.

Scroll phase D (60% to 80%):
- Feature cards stagger in, career spotlight interaction begins.

Scroll phase E (80% to 95%):
- Quiz/certificate proof and product preview video focus.

Scroll phase F (95% to 100%):
- Pricing clarity then final conversion CTA.

---

## 5) Visual Direction (Art Direction Rules)

Visual style:
- Future-learning editorial style.
- Bright, premium, optimistic.
- Avoid generic stock-photo look.

Color strategy:
- Primary blue for trust and intelligence.
- Warm amber for action and highlights.
- Green for progress and achievement.
- Soft neutral backgrounds for section breathing room.

Texture and depth:
- Layered gradients, light grain noise, glass panels, soft shadows.

Typography direction:
- Headline: Space Grotesk or Sora (strong geometric confidence).
- Body: Manrope or Plus Jakarta Sans (high readability).

Motion language:
- Smooth, deliberate, not flashy.
- Scroll-linked narrative movement with clear hierarchy.

## 7) Motion and Interaction Stack (GSAP + Powerful Libraries)

Required motion stack:
- GSAP
- GSAP ScrollTrigger
- GSAP SplitText (or SplitType alternative)
- Lenis (smooth scroll orchestration)
- Framer Motion (micro interactions and small state transitions)

Why this stack:
- GSAP + ScrollTrigger for precise scroll storytelling and timeline control.
- Lenis for fluid scroll experience across devices.
- SplitText/SplitType for premium headline reveals.
- Framer Motion for component-level interactions without overcomplicating global timelines.

Animation rules:
- Respect reduced-motion settings.
- Keep animation durations consistent (0.4s to 1.2s typical).
- Avoid excessive continuous animations that distract from conversion.

---

## 8) Responsive Layout Rules

Desktop (1440 and above):
- Full storytelling with pinned timeline and multi-column sections.

Laptop/Tablet (768 to 1439):
- Simplified pinning, reduced depth effects, preserved section order.

Mobile (below 768):
- One-column stack, swipe testimonial cards, minimal parallax, CTA always reachable.

Critical mobile UX rule:
- Primary CTA must be visible in first viewport without needing precise gestures.
